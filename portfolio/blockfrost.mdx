## The Project

[Blockfrost](https://blockfrost.io/) is a blockchain API provider for Cardano. During my time at Input Output Global, I was assigned for two to three months to assess and improve their documentation. The team had never had a technical writer; documentation had been a side project of the developer team.

I inherited a blank FAQ page.

---

## The Constraint

The signal was in Discord.

Developers and early adopters were having dense technical conversations in the project's Discord channels. Questions, confusion, answers. None of it captured anywhere a future developer could easily find it.

The usual sources weren't there. No support tickets. No user research. No internal docs to repurpose. The engineers were too busy shipping to sit for interviews.

The raw material existed, just in a format no documentation workflow knows how to ingest. Months of backlog across multiple channels. Reading it by hand wasn't viable in the time I had.

---

## The Unconventional Move

I built a tool.

[ChatDoc-InsightMiner-PromptLab](https://github.com/joseph-fajen/ChatDoc-InsightMiner-PromptLab) is a Python toolkit that ingests chat logs into a vector database, runs analytical prompts across three LLMs at once (OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini), and cross-validates the outputs. If all three models independently flag the same pattern, it's probably real. If only one does, it may be an artifact.

I fed it months of Discord history and asked it to find the recurring themes, questions and points of confusion.

**Result:** [platform.blockfrost.io/faq](https://platform.blockfrost.io/faq)

## The Broader Pattern

Most documentation starts after someone decides what to document. Here, I had to figure that out first.

The toolkit is open source and works for any project with community conversation data.
